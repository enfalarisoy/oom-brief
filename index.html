Brief Companion Page

Oops! Out of Moves

A brief introduction to dynamic post-failure offers in mobile games
Enfal Arisoy & Ovunc Yilmaz · Leeds School of Business, CU Boulder
1. Why Games Matter

Mobile games are a huge and growing business

Video games are one of the fastest-growing entertainment categories, with projected industry revenue approaching $400 billion by 2027. Mobile games account for half of the industry revenue.

As mobile game industry shifts from ad-driven models to in-app-purchase driven revenue models, understanding how in-game offers affect player behavior becomes increasingly important.

2. Match-3 Monetization

Post-failure bonus action offers are core monetization tools

A central monetization tool in casual mobile games is post-failure bonus action offers. These are bonus actions such as extra moves/lives offered for in-game currency (i.e., coins) after a failure and allow players to continue from the point of failure without restarting.

3. Policy Change

The industry moved from fixed offers to dynamic offers

Under the fixed policy, players receive the same post-failure offer each time they fail. Under the dynamic policy, offer size increases with repeated failures on the same level (+5, +5, +10, +15, ...), while offer prices remain unchanged.

4. Trade-Off

Why this design change could help or hurt revenue

The key question is intertemporal: does a larger offer increase monetization, or does it simply pull spending forward while reducing future purchase opportunities?

Expansion
Players might be more willing to accept larger offers, which can increase the monetization.
Policy shift
Fixed → Dynamic
More moves per purchase
Acceleration
Larger bundles can help players clear levels faster, reducing exposure to the post-failure offers therefore reduce monetization.

Main takeaway
Dynamic post-failure offers create an immediate increase in spending, but that lift fades quickly and turns into a persistent decline. A short A/B test would likely overstate profitability.

Brief Companion Page For

Oops! Out of Moves

The Impact of Dynamic Post-Failure Offers on Monetization in Mobile Games
Enfal Arisoy & Ovunc Yilmaz · Leeds School of Business, CU Boulder
1. Why Games Matter

Mobile games are a huge and growing business

Video games are one of the fastest-growing entertainment categories, with projected industry revenue approaching $400 billion by 2027.

As mobile game industry shifts from ad-driven models to in-app-purchase driven revenue models, understanding how in-game offers affect player behavior becomes increasingly important.

2. Match-3 Monetization

Post-failure bonus action offers are core monetization tools

First, how these games work
1
At each level, players need to clear the obstacles in the board by making matches of three or more identical objects with a limited number of moves—for example, 27 moves.
2
If players clear all obstacles before their moves run out, they complete the level and advance to the next level.
3
If players run out of moves before clearing the board, they fail. Right at that failure moment, the game shows an offer: spend coins to get a few extra moves and continue from where they got stuck, instead of starting over.

That offer—shown at the moment of failure—is what we call as a post-failure bonus action offer, and it is a central monetization tool in casual mobile games. These offers provide bonus actions such as extra moves or lives in exchange for in-game currency (i.e., coins), letting players continue from the point of failure without restarting.

3. Policy Change

Starting in 2023, leading casual games began shifting from fixed to dynamic post-failure offers.

Under the fixed policy, players receive the same post-failure offer each time they fail. Under the dynamic policy, offer size increases with repeated failures on the same level (+5, +5, +10, +15, ...), while offer prices remain unchanged.

4. Trade-Off

Research Question:

How does switching from a fixed to a dynamic post-failure offer policy affect players' intertemporal spending?

Expansion
Players might be more willing to accept larger offers, which can increase the monetization.
Acceleration
Larger bundles can help players clear levels faster, reducing exposure to the post-failure offers therefore reduce monetization.

7. Main Takeaways
  1. "Helping more" can reduce monetization!
    • Larger post-failure offers decrease spending among hardcore players.
    • Opposite to common industry practice.
    • Future step: other players and churn behavior.
  2. Short-run A/B tests ≠ Long-run truth!
    • A/B tests overestimate long-run profitability for this policy.
    • They miss intertemporal substitution in purchases, which is critical in micro-transaction environments.
  3. Personalization matters!
    • Do not use or overuse for high-skill players.
    • Use selectively for low-skill players.
    • Condition on context: very easy or very hard levels.
